GPU-Collateralized Debt Facilities: The Rise of Chip Loans
Bryan Gibson  ·  Brandon Hall  ·  Peter Demerjian   |   Interim Research Presentation · April 2026
Research Question
Do existing accounting standards adequately measure the depreciation, revenue recognition, and leverage risk embedded in GPU-collateralized debt?
Problem Setting
  • $35B in GPU-backed debt originated 2023–2025 with no established accounting precedent
  • Hardware depreciates 18-month cycles; ASC 360-10 written for durable physical plant
  • Lenders price covenant compliance on EBITDA metrics that management can shift via useful-life elections
Literature Gap
  • Vendor-financing collapses (Lucent, GE Capital) show collateral value risk is systematic, not idiosyncratic
  • No prior study measures ASC 360 / 606 / 842 interaction in technology-collateralized debt
  • BIS (2026) flags AI-financing as an emerging macro-financial risk with no measurement framework
Conceptual Framing
  • ASC 360-10 useful-life discretion as an earnings management lever (Watts & Zimmerman 1986)
  • ASC 842 commencement-date rule as off-balance-sheet leverage channel
  • Shleifer & Vishny (1992) fire-sale liquidation: no secondary GPU market implies near-zero recovery
Research Method
  • Single case study: CoreWeave FY2025 10-K, S-1, 8-K covenant amendments
  • Scenario analysis across 2yr / 4yr / 6yr useful-life elections · ROIC vs. 11.13% WACC
  • Lease capitalization: implied borrowing rate applied to $38.5B uncommenced leases

Three Accounting Measurement Gaps ·Paper Findings
1
Depreciation Divergence ·ASC 360-10
Useful life estimates span 2 to 6 years across identical hardware. $2.7B annual depreciation gap exceeds EBITDA. All recent changes trend toward longer lives ·consistent with earnings management. ROIC negative in every scenario vs. 11.13% WACC.
2
Revenue Recognition Gap ·ASC 606 / ASC 718
AMD warrant grants to Meta & OpenAI (320M shares, $0.01 strike) tied to chip purchase milestones raise an unresolved ASC 606-10-32-25 question. Upper-bound revenue reduction: $10.3B / yr (32% of recognized revenue). FASB has not yet acted.
3
Off-Balance-Sheet Leverage ·ASC 842
$38.5B uncommenced data center leases disclosed but not recognized (commencement timing rule). Capitalizing at implied borrowing rate adds $22B to invested capital ·nearly doubling reported leverage. ROIC compresses to –3.8% in the most favorable scenario.
CoreWeave Debt Maturity ·$21.6B Total, FCF –$7.2B/yr
Dec 2025 covenant amendments: DSCR testing deferred to Oct 2027, liquidity floor cut to $100M, unlimited equity cures. CDS spreads doubled since Oct 2025. EBIT/interest = –0.04×. Senior notes trading above coupon.
