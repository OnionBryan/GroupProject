GPU-Collateralized Debt Facilities: The Rise of Chip Loans
Bryan Gibson  ·  Brandon Hall  ·  Peter Demerjian   |   Interim Research Presentation · April 2026
Research Question
Do existing accounting standards adequately measure the depreciation, revenue recognition, and leverage risk embedded in GPU-collateralized debt?
Problem Setting
  • $35B in GPU-backed debt originated 2023–2025 with no established accounting precedent
  • Vendor-financing collapses (Lucent, GE Capital) show collateral value risk is systematic, not idiosyncratic
  • Lenders price covenant compliance on EBITDA metrics management can shift via useful-life elections
Literature Gap
  • Hardware depreciates on 18-month cycles; ASC 360-10 was written for durable physical plant — standards lag asset class
  • No prior study examines ASC 360 / 606 / 842 interaction in technology-collateralized debt
  • BIS (2026) flags AI-financing as an emerging macro-financial risk with no measurement framework

Conceptual Framing
  • Watts & Zimmerman (1986) Positive Accounting Theory: managers select useful-life estimates to maximize EBITDA and satisfy DSCR covenant thresholds
  • Shleifer & Vishny (1992) asset specificity: GPU collateral has no deep secondary market · industry-wide distress produces near-zero liquidation recovery
  • Jensen & Meckling (1976) agency costs of debt: information asymmetry between lenders and borrowers on collateral value enables systematic understatement of leverage
Research Method
  • Single case study: CoreWeave FY2025 10-K, S-1, 8-K covenant amendments
  • Scenario analysis across 2yr / 4yr / 6yr useful-life elections · ROIC vs. 11.13% WACC
  • Lease capitalization: implied borrowing rate applied to $38.5B uncommenced leases
